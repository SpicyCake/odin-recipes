Mochi

Description

Mochi are small, sweet Japanese cakes made with glutinous rice flour (mochiko). They have a soft, chewy outer layer and a deliciously sticky filling made of sweetened red bean paste. Freezing the red bean paste before you use it is optional but it does make the process easier.

Ingredient list

  • 1 Cup of Red Bean Paste
  • 1 Cup of Glutinous Rice Flour
  • 1 Teaspoon of Green Tea Powder
  • 1 Cup of Water
  • 1/4 Cup of Sugar
  • 1/2 Cup of Cornstarch

Steps

  1. Freeze bean paste
    Wrap in foil and freeze for one hour
  2. Mix rice flour with green tea powder
    Be sure to use large mircowave safe bowl
  3. Mix in water with the flour mixture
    Mix till smooth
  4. Mix in sugar with flour mixture
    Mix till smooth
  5. Wrap bowl in plastic wrap and microwave for 3 minutes and 30 seconds
  6. Remove bean paste from fridge
    Seperate the paste into 8 equal balls
  7. Remove mochi from microwave and stir
  8. Recover mochi and heat again for 15-30 seconds
  9. Dust a working surface with cornstarch
  10. Scoop out about 2 tablespoons of hot mochi and form into balls
  11. Flatten the mochi balls onto the dusted surface
  12. Place balls of bean paste into the flattened mochi balls
  13. Pinch the mochi around the ball of bean paste to seal it in
  14. Dust the completed balls and enjoy
